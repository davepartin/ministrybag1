## Curriculum Design Notes
*Added March 2026 - from Dave and Claude curriculum conversation*

### Position in the 202 arc
202.04, 202.05, and 202.06 form a three-lesson sequence that should be built and read as a unit. The sequence moves from map to story to method:

- 202.04: Knowing Your Bible - the map. Before you take a road trip, you need to know how the road is laid out.
- 202.05: The Biblical Storyline - the road trip. Now that you have the map, this lesson takes you on the journey.
- 202.06: How to Study the Bible - the skill. Now that you know where you are in the story, here is how to dig deeper into any passage.

This is a deliberate arc. Do not build these as three independent lessons. Build them as one journey that unfolds across three lessons.

### Why this belongs at 202 and not 101
The learner arriving at 202.04 has already completed 101 (7 lessons) and 201 (10 lessons). They have read most of the Gospel of John, learned to pray, been through foundational gospel theology, and encountered the Holy Spirit. They are not a blank slate. They are ready to understand the Bible as a whole book rather than a collection of individual passages.

A new believer in 101 is not ready for a Bible overview. They need one passage and one method. Give them that in 101.04. Save the big picture for here, when they have enough biblical experience to appreciate it.

### What makes this lesson succeed
The learner needs to walk away feeling that the Bible is not a chaotic collection of books and laws and poems. It is one unified library with a structure they can learn. The bookshelf analogy from the TOC (Law, History, Poetry, Prophets, Gospels, Epistles) is strong. Keep it simple and visual. A phone-friendly infographic or interactive element would serve this lesson well.

### Connection to 202.06
This lesson is the foundation for 202.06. The learner needs to know how the Bible is organized before they can study it well. A person who does not know what kind of literature they are reading (poetry, prophecy, epistle, narrative) will misinterpret it. That is why the overview comes first.

### Key story candidate
Josiah finding the Book of the Law (2 Kings 22) is a powerful story for this lesson. A king who had never heard the Word of God, hearing it for the first time, and being completely undone by it. The power of simply encountering Scripture. Consider whether to use this here or save it for 202.06.
