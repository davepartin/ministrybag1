# 201.07: The Biblical Storyline

## Overview
**Key Verse:** Revelation 21:5
**Description:** Creation, Fall, Redemption, Restoration - the four-chapter story of the Bible with Jesus at the center.
**Goal:** Trace God's redemptive story from creation through Scripture and see how Jesus is the climax of all history.

## Curriculum Design Notes
*Added March 2026 - from Dave and Claude curriculum conversation*

### Position in the 202 arc
202.05 is the middle lesson of the three-lesson Bible sequence (202.04 - 202.05 - 202.06). By this point the learner has the map of Scripture (202.04). Now this lesson takes them on the road trip. See 202.04 research notes for the full arc description.

### The four-act framework
Creation, Fall, Redemption, Restoration is the engine of this lesson. This is not new content at the 202 level - the learner has already encountered the gospel, sin, and justification in 201. What is new here is seeing those realities as part of one unified story that spans all of Scripture. The 201 series teaches the doctrines. This lesson shows how those doctrines fit into a narrative arc.

The goal is that the learner finishes this lesson and can hold their Bible in their hand and say: "I know what this book is doing. I know where it is going. And I know where I am in the story."

### The Road to Emmaus (Luke 24) as the key story
Luke 24:13-35 is the strongest possible story for this lesson. Two disciples, shattered by the crucifixion, walking away from Jerusalem. Jesus joins them and walks them through Moses and all the Prophets, explaining everything in the Scriptures concerning Himself. Their hearts burned within them. This is the best picture in the entire Bible of what understanding the biblical storyline feels like. Use it.

This also reinforces that the Old Testament is not a separate book with a different God. It is the same story, pointing forward to Jesus. That is a crucial correction for many new believers who mentally separate the Testaments.

### BibleProject connection
BibleProject has exceptional free video content on the biblical storyline. Their "Story of the Bible" video and their individual book overview videos are world-class. Consider linking to them directly in the lesson or in the bible_reading section. They use the same four-act framework (Creation, Fall, Redemption, Restoration) and would reinforce this lesson naturally.

### What this lesson is not
This lesson is not a timeline of events or a survey of every book of the Bible. That is what 202.04 does. This lesson is about the one story underneath all the stories. Keep it focused on the arc. The learner does not need to remember dates or kings. They need to see Jesus at the center of everything.

### Key one-liner candidate
"Every promise in the Old Testament is a shadow. Jesus is the sun that casts it." This captures the Christ-centered reading of Scripture without becoming academic. Consider using it as the key line for this lesson.
