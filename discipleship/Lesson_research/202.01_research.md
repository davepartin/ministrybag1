**Protect the Source of Love — 1 John 4:19**
This lesson must not become a lesson about trying harder to love difficult people. The theological anchor underneath the second Greatest Commandment is the same as the first: *"We love because he first loved us"* (1 John 4:19). The whole 201 series established that loving God is a response to His initiating love — and loving neighbors is the overflow of that same love, not a separate effort generated from willpower.

The danger in 202.01 is that it drifts into moral instruction: "be nicer, serve more, consider others better than yourself." Those are true but they collapse without the engine underneath them. The lesson needs to establish early that neighbor-love is not a command to manufacture love you don't have — it is the natural outflow of someone who has received the Father's love, been adopted through the Son, and is walking by the Spirit. Those are the first three lessons of 201. This lesson is where that love turns outward.

A simple way to protect this: open the lesson by asking *"Where does the love come from?"* before asking *"Who is my neighbor?"* Answer the source question first (1 John 4:19, 4:11 — "since God so loved us, we also ought to love one another"), then the scope question (the Good Samaritan). The love flows from being loved — it doesn't originate in us.

**Curriculum Thread to Preserve**
201.01 established: we love God because He first loved us (Echo of Love — Receive → Respond → Remain).
202.01 must complete the arc: we love neighbors because we have received that same love and it now overflows outward. The source never changes. Only the direction shifts — from vertical (God) to horizontal (others).
