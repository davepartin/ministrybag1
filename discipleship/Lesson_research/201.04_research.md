# 201.04: Spiritual Disciplines — Abiding in Christ

## Overview
**Key Verse:** John 15:4-5
**Description:** Building sustainable rhythms of prayer, Scripture, fellowship, stewardship, and fighting sin as ways of staying connected to Jesus. Includes practical guidance on daily devotional habits as the daily rhythm that keeps the disciplines alive.
**Goal:** Understand disciplines as means of abiding in Christ — not self-improvement — assess your current rhythm honestly, and commit to one or two concrete new practices.

---

## Theological Anchor — Protect This
*(The guardrail that keeps this lesson from becoming a self-improvement class)*

> *"The spiritual disciplines are those practices found in Scripture that promote spiritual growth among believers in the gospel of Jesus Christ. They are habits of devotion, habits of experiential Christianity that have been practiced by God's people since biblical times."*
> — Donald Whitney, *Spiritual Disciplines for the Christian Life*

This is the definitional anchor — use it early to establish what the disciplines are and where they come from. Then pivot immediately to the purpose:

> *"We are holy, under the righteous covering of Jesus, but we are also commanded to seek holiness and godliness. The purpose of being spiritually disciplined is to increase our intimacy with and dependency on Jesus."*
> — Dave Partin, Leadership Course

This framing must open the lesson. The disciplines are not how we earn God's favor — they are how we stay close to the One who already loves us. Abiding (John 15) is the framework. The disciplines are the means of abiding, not the goal themselves.

---

## The Core Metaphor — Must Use
*(Connects directly to the Growing Together curriculum name)*

**"Fertilizer for the Roots"**
These five disciplines are *fertilizer for the roots* of one's personal relationship with Jesus. You don't water the leaves — you feed the roots. Healthy roots produce everything else: fruit, strength, resilience in drought. The disciplines don't produce the fruit directly. They keep the roots connected to the Vine (John 15), and the Vine produces the fruit.

This metaphor is worth drawing or illustrating. It also threads back to the curriculum name — Growing Together — and forward to 202.08 (Fruit of the Spirit as the evidence of healthy roots).

## The Five Disciplines
*(From Dave's Leadership Course — confirmed framework for this lesson)*

### 1. Prayer
**The frame:** A conversation — talking with and listening to God. Prayer displays our dependence on a powerful and loving Father.

**Teaching tool — ACTS Pattern** *(from Matthew 6:5-15, the Lord's Prayer)*
- **A**doration — worship and acknowledge who God is
- **C**onfession — honest acknowledgment of sin
- **T**hanksgiving — gratitude for what He has done
- **S**upplication — bringing requests to God

**Scope of prayer:** Families, friends, leaders, enemies. Salvation of the lost, healing, boldness, return of Jesus, the church, other churches.

**Key Verse:**
*Ephesians 6:18-20 — "Pray in the Spirit on all occasions with all kinds of prayers and requests... always keep on praying for all the Lord's people."*

---

### 2. Bible Study
**The frame:** The Bible is God's Story — His Story, History. It is God's love letter to us, teaching and reminding us of Creation, Fall, Redemption, and Restoration. God is the pursuer; we are the rebel.

**Teaching tool — Six Ways to Engage Scripture:**
Read · Meditate · Memorize · Apply · Study · Teach

*(Note: Session 4 of the leadership course is focused on studying and teaching — this lesson introduces the practices, that lesson goes deeper)*

**Key Verses:**
*Psalm 119:9-11 — "How can a young man keep his way pure? By guarding it according to your word... I have stored up your word in my heart, that I might not sin against you."*

*Psalm 119:105 — "Your word is a lamp to my feet and a light to my path."*

---

### 3. Fellowship
**The frame:** We need each other. The church is a community of believers, not a collection of individuals. We are made to love, care, serve, support, encourage, rebuke, and forgive one another.

**Key Verse:**
*Hebrews 10:24-25 — "Let us consider how to stir up one another to love and good works, not neglecting to meet together... but encouraging one another."*

*(Note: This verse also appears in 301.01 for accountability — the connection is intentional. Fellowship and accountability overlap here.)*

---

### 4. Stewardship
**The frame:** Everything is God's. Our Time, Talents, and Treasure need to be understood in that reality. Giving is an act of trust, worship, and faith — it gives God glory as we grow in our dependence on Him.

**The Three T's:** Time · Talents · Treasure *(simple framework for teaching the scope of stewardship)*

**Key Verse:**
*2 Corinthians 9:6-7 — "Whoever sows sparingly will also reap sparingly, and whoever sows generously will also reap generously... God loves a cheerful giver."*

*(Note: 201.10 goes deeper on giving and generosity. This lesson introduces stewardship as a discipline; 201.10 focuses on the heart behind giving.)*

---

### 5. Fighting Sin
**The frame:** We are to put on the armor of God to fight in spiritual warfare. We are to flee temptations, resist the Devil, set our minds on things above and not of this earth, and fear the Lord so as to turn away from evil.

**Key Verse:**
*1 Peter 2:11-12 — "Beloved, I urge you as sojourners and exiles to abstain from the passions of the flesh, which wage war against your soul. Keep your conduct among the Gentiles honorable..."*

*(Note: 202.09 goes deep on purity and fighting temptation. This discipline establishes the posture; that lesson provides the practical strategy.)*

## A Note on John 15:1-17 — The Full Passage
The key verse (15:4-5) anchors the abiding framework, but the full passage extends further and is worth teaching in full context:
- **v.1-8:** Abiding, fruit, pruning — the disciplines framework
- **v.9-11:** Abiding in love, keeping commandments as joy not burden — connects to 201.01 (Father's love) and the Key Line "things of God are no longer burdensome but joy"
- **v.12-17:** Love one another, friendship with Jesus, being chosen — this is the bridge from 201.04 into 202.01 (Loving Your Neighbors). The disciplines are not just personal growth — they flow outward into love for others. Abiding produces fruit. Fruit includes love for people.

Worth noting when teaching: John 15 doesn't let the disciplines stay private. Verse 12 arrives immediately after the abiding teaching — "This is my commandment, that you love one another as I have loved you." The disciplines are the roots; love for others is part of the fruit.

## Teaching Technique — The Modeling Question
*(From leadership course, question 3.2 — use as an early reflection prompt before instruction)*

Before teaching the five disciplines, ask learners: *"Write about someone in your life who has been a good example of someone who practices spiritual disciplines. Why were they a good example, and how did this affect their everyday life?"*

This works because people learn disciplines better from watching someone than from a framework. The question is relational and concrete before it's instructional. It also surfaces what people already believe about what a disciplined life looks like — which lets you correct misconceptions before they harden.

**A theological note worth naming in the lesson:** Spiritual disciplines done rightly produce humility, not pride. The person who genuinely abides looks more dependent on God, not more impressive to people. If someone is using the disciplines to feel spiritually superior, something has gone wrong at the root. The warning against spiritual pride belongs in this lesson — quietly but clearly.

---

## The Self-Assessment Teaching Tool
*(The personal scoring exercise from the leadership course — use as a discussion/reflection component)*

At the end of the lesson, invite learners to score themselves 1-10 in each of the five disciplines — not to shame them, but to give them honest self-awareness and a starting point. The questions that drive this:

1. *Score yourself 1-10 in each of the five disciplines and explain your score.*
2. *If you were a 10 in each discipline, what would your relationship with Jesus look like?*
3. *Choose at least 2 disciplines and write down 2-3 growth action steps you'll take in the next 2 weeks.*

The scoring exercise works because it's personal and specific — not "how's your spiritual life?" but "what's your prayer score and why?" That specificity creates honest conversation.
