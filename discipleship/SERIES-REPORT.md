# Growing Together Discipleship Series
## A Report on Vision, Design, and Purpose

*Neighborhood Church - Overland Park, Kansas*
*Dave Partin, Pastor and Author*

---

## What This Is

Growing Together is a 47-lesson self-paced discipleship series built for a phone screen, written for a new believer, and designed to be used in community. It is not a textbook, a curriculum package, or a church program. It is a path - a single, coherent journey from the first questions of new faith all the way to the point where someone is helping another person follow Jesus.

The series is organized into five courses, each built around the image of a growing tree. The 101 course lays down roots. The 201 course builds the trunk strong. The 202 and 203 courses grow branches and bear fruit. The 301 course watches one tree become a forest. That is not metaphor borrowed from somewhere else - it is the actual architecture of the series. Every lesson, every tool, every visual exists inside that single image, and it carries a thesis that becomes a compass for the whole project: "We grow deep so we can grow up so we can grow out."

---

## Why This Series

Most discipleship resources fall into one of two failure modes. The first is curriculum that is academically strong but practically thin - the person finishes knowing more theology but has no idea how to live it or pass it on. The second is devotional material that is warm and readable but has no spine - it encourages without equipping, and the learner ends up spiritually nourished in the moment but not formed for the long haul.

Growing Together was built to avoid both. The material is written in plain language at a readability level accessible to someone with limited reading confidence, but the theology underneath it is not shallow. The goal is not to simplify truth but to remove the unnecessary barriers between a person and the truth. A new believer does not need easier doctrine. They need doctrine written in a language they can receive.

The other reason this series exists is reproducibility. Most discipleship resources are designed to be consumed. This one is designed to be handed off. Every lesson is structured so that the person who goes through it once can eventually take someone else through it. The frameworks, the tools, the diagrams, the questions - they are all built with three passes in mind. First pass: you receive it. Second pass: you take someone else through it and chew on it yourself. Third pass: you teach it two or three times until it has become part of how you see the world.

That is what separates this from a Bible study. A Bible study produces informed Christians. This series aims to produce disciple-makers.

---

## Why It Is Written This Way

The pastoral voice of this series is deliberate. Every lesson is written as if a caring pastor is sitting across the table from one person - not preaching at them, not lecturing through them, but walking beside them. The language is direct without being cold. It is clear without being thin. It carries warmth without becoming soft on truth.

The formatting choices flow from the same conviction. No bullet points in lesson prose. Short paragraphs. One main idea at a time. Varied sentence rhythm. Plain words where plain words will do. These are not stylistic preferences - they are theological ones. The idea is that how something is said communicates as much as what is said. If the writing feels like a lecture, it will position the reader as a student. If it feels like a conversation, it will position the reader as a person being invited into something real.

The series also makes heavy use of tools, frameworks, and diagrams. Every lesson where it is appropriate includes something the learner can hold onto - a visual cycle, an interactive diagram, a memory phrase, an illustrated parable. These are not decoration. They are the mechanism of reproducibility. A person cannot pass on an outline, but they can pass on a picture. They can hand someone their phone and say, "Work through this diagram." The tools make the content portable.

Scripture is woven throughout, not stacked at the front. The goal is for the learner to discover that the Bible is not a source of proof texts but the living document through which God speaks and acts. By the time someone finishes this series, they should have a felt sense that the whole Bible is one unified story moving toward Jesus.

## What It Is Building Toward

The anchor verse for the entire series is Colossians 2:6-7: "As you received Christ Jesus the Lord, so walk in him, rooted and built up in him and established in the faith." That passage describes a person, not a program. The person is someone who started by receiving - not performing, not producing, just receiving. And then walking. And then growing roots, building up, being established.

That is the arc. The series asks: what does a person need to move from "I just said yes to Jesus" to "I am a steady, rooted, mission-minded follower of Christ who can help someone else do the same"? And then it tries to answer that question honestly, in the right order, with the right tools.

The goal is not a graduate. The goal is a disciple-maker. That distinction shapes every editorial decision in the project. It is why the series does not pile on theological content faster than a person can digest it. It is why every lesson ends with three simple questions - what did I learn, what will I do this week, what is a prayer request - questions that are simple enough to answer alone and direct enough to share in a five-minute conversation with a mentor. It is why the 301 course does not begin until the learner has done the work of the first four courses, because leadership built on an untransformed life produces exactly the kind of Christian leader the church already has too many of.

## How It Will Change Communities

Discipleship is the mechanism by which the church reproduces itself. Not programs. Not services. Not platforms. People who know Jesus and know how to help someone else know him. If that mechanism is broken or absent, the church can be large and active and still be one generation from extinction. If it is healthy, a church of fifty people can have more lasting impact than a church of five thousand.

Growing Together is designed to be the on-ramp to that mechanism. It is written and built so that a church plant with no staff budget, no curriculum director, and no classroom space can put this in the hands of a new believer on a Sunday morning and say, "Start here." And then six months later that person knows enough to put it in the hands of someone they care about and say the same thing.

When that happens at scale - when twenty people become forty, and forty become eighty, not by attendance but by personal disciple-making - the texture of a neighborhood begins to change. Not because a church ran a campaign, but because ordinary people are walking around with a clear gospel, a personal story, and the tools to share both. That is the vision behind this series. Not growth as an institutional metric. Growth as an organic reality - one person, one conversation, one disciple at a time.

## Current Status

As of spring 2026, the series is in active development. The full 101 course (7 lessons) is complete and refined, with attention to readability, sentence rhythm, and pastoral tone. The 201 course (10 lessons, the theological core of the series) is substantially built, with several lessons in advanced draft and the key anchor lessons fully written. The 202, 203, and 301 courses have lesson structures and research in place.

The app itself is a single-file web application designed for phone screens, with no external dependencies, full offline capability for content, auto-saving responses to local storage, interactive widgets embedded directly in lesson flow, and a visual design built around warm earth tones and the tree metaphor. It is clean enough to feel trustworthy and simple enough to feel accessible.

The project is ongoing. Dave Partin does final theological review on every lesson. The editorial standard is high by design, because the people who will use this deserve material that takes them seriously.
